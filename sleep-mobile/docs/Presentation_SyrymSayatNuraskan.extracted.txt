Project Summary
Objective: Development of an intelligent mobile system for objective
monitoring of sleep quality and stress levels using standard
smartphone sensors.
 
Technology Stack:                           
                 Backend: Java Spring Boot (REST API, Security). 
                 Frontend: Cross-platform mobile app (Flutter/React Native). 
                 AI Service: Python (TensorFlow/PyTorch) for data analysis.
Key Innovation: Unlike manual trackers, the system uses passive
data collection (accelerometer, microphone) and Machine Learning to
identify non-obvious correlations between lifestyle and physical state.
 
Outcome: A scalable architecture that transforms raw sensor data
into personalized health recommendations.

===== PAGE 5 =====

I N T R O D U C T I O N In the modern fast-paced world, chronic stress and
sleep disorders have become critical issues affecting
physical and mental health. While people often
experience fatigue and anxiety, they lack accessible
tools for an objective assessment of their condition.
Existing solutions are either too subjective or
require expensive medical equipment, making the
monitoring process opaque and inconsistent.
The goal of this work is to design and develop a
mobile software system that utilizes artificial
intelligence for automated, data-driven analysis of
sleep patterns and stress levels. By leveraging
passive sensor data (microphone, accelerometer),
the system aims to provide transparent decision
support in the form of personalized
recommendations, helping users improve their well-
being and form healthier daily habits.

P R O B L E M 
S T A T E M E N T 
The Diagnostic Gap
Clinical Limitations: Polysomnography (PSG) is
accurate but expensive, invasive, and
uncomfortable for patients.
Consumer Limitations: Wearables and apps
are accessible but often lack diagnostic accuracy
and fail to provide clinical-grade insights.
The "Siloed" Modeling Problem
Most current solutions model sleep and stress in
isolation.
They ignore the clinically established
bidirectional relationship between sleep
quality and chronic stress.
The Technological Gap
Advanced architectures like Transformers,
which excel at long-term time series analysis, are
largely unexplored in consumer health apps.
Privacy and Explainable AI (XAI) are often treated
as afterthoughts, not core features.

RESULTS AND 
FUTURE WORK
CURRENT PROGRESS
Data Quality: Addressing the challenge of
ensuring high data quality from non-
medical, consumer-grade phone sensors
(microphone, accelerometer).
Modeling Gap: The theoretical necessity
of moving beyond simple analysis to
jointly model the bidirectional sleep-
stress axis.
Architecture Validation: Justifying the
selection of Spring Boot and PostgreSQL
over alternatives to ensure robust
security and scalability for health data.
FUTURE WORK
Security Hardening: Implement TLS 1.3
for data in transit and BCrypt for
credentials.
Privacy Engine: Develop a mechanism for
immediate deletion of raw audio (Data
Minimization).
Advanced AI: Research and integrate
Federated Learning to decentralize model
training.
Validation: Conduct end-to-end testing
with real users to verify diagnostic
accuracy.
